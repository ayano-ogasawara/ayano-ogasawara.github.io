My journey towards User Experience

Like many, I have discovered and experimented a lot with the tools that came along with the digital age! I wrote my first website when I was 10 and when IE was cool, I discovered photoshop when it was still working offline and I especially enjoyed messing around with music.

After graduating university in 2012 I worked in a hostel during the day and as a DJ during the night, in Tokyo, Japan. In both jobs the customer was the main focus and I made sure to keep it that way. During my tenure the hostel business enjoyed an excellent rating across the multiple branches I helped manage / open (9 our of 10). And I was lucky enough to be able to DJ in small, medium and bigger parties in Japan and overseas.

Then in 2016 I decided I would have a carreer in IT. Being familiar with management thanks to my hostel experience, I became a project manager officer. There I could use my communication and presentation skills to the fullest until I moved to Berlin in 2017 and joined a startup (as a PM/PO) where I would eventually work with a UX designer that made a strong impression on me! The discipline seemed to encapsulate A LOT of what I wanted to do: create, learn, challenge expectations, seek truth rather than opinions and most of all really care about the customer.

So today, having some development and project management experience on top of having graduated Carreer Foundry's UX/UI Certification gives me a very strong basis on which to build upon.
